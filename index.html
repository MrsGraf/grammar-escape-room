The Briefing

For investigation purposes only

During renovation work at your school, construction workers discover a rusted metal box buried beneath the old gym floor.

Inside: letters, photographs, and diary entries left by three teenagers — Emma, Tom, and Lena — who sealed the capsule in June 2006. But the box is locked with a combination lock.

To open it and set their memories free, your team must solve six grammar challenges hidden in their documents. Each challenge reveals one digit of the code.

The clock is ticking. The past is waiting to be found.

How to play

  • Work as a team and discuss every answer.
  • Open the six case files in any order.
  • Fill in a puzzle and check your answers.
  • When everything is correct, count to find the digit and lock it in.
  • Collect all 6 digits, then enter the code on the lock.
  • Stuck? Open the Hint Envelope on any puzzle.

Congratulations

Inside the capsule, beneath the letters and photographs, you find a final note on pale blue paper, the ink slightly faded:

"If you're reading this — hello from the past. We were sixteen when we buried this box. Emma, Tom, and Lena. It was June 14th, 2006, and we had just finished our exams. We were scared of the future: of leaving, of changing, of losing each other.

So we buried our fears and our hopes here, under the oak tree, behind the gym. And we wrote one question on the inside of the box lid: 'Does it get better?'

Look at yourselves. You found this. You solved every challenge. You worked together, argued, thought, and didn't give up. That's your answer.

Yes. It gets better. Complicated, uncertain, surprising — but better.

The real time capsule was never the box. It was this moment: you, reading our words, eighteen years later. We made it to your future. Now go make yours."

— Emma, Tom & Lena · June 14, 2006 —
